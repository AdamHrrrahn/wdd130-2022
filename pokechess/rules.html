General Rules

  • This is a two-player chess game.
  • Play Starts with Player 1, and alternates bewtween each player making one move.
  • Player 1's pieces start at the top of hte board, and player 2's pieces start at the bottom.
  • Each turn the current player must make one valid move. possible moves for each piece are described below.
  • A move is valid if:
    1. The target space is unocupied or occupied by an opposing piece.
    2. After the move is made, the current player is not in check.
    3. advanced moves such as castling or en passant are not considered valid for this game.
  • If a piece ends its move on the same space as an opposing piece, that piece is 'taken' and removed from the game.
  • If the current player is in check and has no valid moves, they are in checkmate and lose the game.
  • If the current player is not in check and has no valid moves, the game is a stalemate, or draw.
  • At any time, the game can be reset by cliking the new game button or refreshing the page.

King

The King may move one space horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. The King may not move into check.

Queen

A queen may move any number of spaces horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. A queen may not move through other pieces.

Rook

A rook may move any number of spaces horizontally, or vertically. A rook may not move through other pieces.

Bishop

A bishop may move any number of spaces diagonally. The bishop may not move through other pieces.

Knight

The knight can choose one of the following movement options:

  • The knight may move two spaces horizontally followed by one space vertically
  • The knight may move two spaces vertically followed by one space horizontally

The knight may pass through other pieces. only The opposing piece at the final destination is taken.
